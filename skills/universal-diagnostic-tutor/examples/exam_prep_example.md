# Exam Prep Example

## User Question

I keep missing multiple-choice questions where two answers both seem right. How
do I choose?

## Diagnosis

- Subject: Exam prep.
- Topic: Multiple-choice reasoning and distractor analysis.
- Prerequisite: Reading the question stem, identifying the tested skill, and
  comparing answer choices against evidence.
- Likely gap: Choosing a true statement instead of the statement that best
  answers the exact question.

## Example Answer Structure

1. Explain that multiple-choice exams often include tempting true-but-wrong
   answers.
2. Teach the rule: answer the question asked, not a nearby question.
3. Show a small generic example with two plausible choices.
4. Give a step-by-step elimination method.
5. Point out common traps: extreme wording, partial truth, wrong scope, and
   irrelevant accuracy.
6. Give a short practice question.

## Why This Is Diagnosis-First

The answer diagnoses the exam-taking pattern behind the mistakes, rather than
only solving one question.
