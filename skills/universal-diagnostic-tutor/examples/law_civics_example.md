# Law And Civics Example

## User Question

What is the difference between civil law and criminal law? I always mix them
up.

## Diagnosis

- Subject: Law and civics.
- Topic: Civil vs criminal legal systems.
- Prerequisite: Difference between a private dispute and a public offense.
- Likely gap: The learner may be sorting cases by seriousness instead of by who
  brings the case and what remedy is sought.

## Teaching Depth Level

Level 3: Standard teacher-style explanation.

## Example Answer

This is a law/civics distinction. The key starting point is not "serious vs not
serious." It is **who brings the case and what the legal system is trying to
do**.

Civil law usually handles disputes between people, companies, or organizations.
The goal is often to fix harm through money, an order to do something, or an
order to stop doing something. For example, if a tenant sues a landlord over an
unreturned deposit, that is usually civil.

Criminal law involves the government accusing someone of breaking a criminal
rule. The goal is punishment, public safety, or deterrence. For example, if the
state charges someone with theft, that is criminal.

A useful test is: **Who is bringing the case?** If it is one private party
against another, think civil. If it is the government prosecuting an offense,
think criminal.

Common mistake: A case can feel emotionally serious and still be civil. The
category depends on the legal structure, not just how bad the situation feels.

Check yourself: If a company sues another company for breaking a contract, is
that civil or criminal?

## Why This Is Diagnosis-First

The answer identifies the learner's likely sorting mistake before giving the
definitions.

## What An Answer-First Assistant Would Miss

It would list definitions without explaining the mental test: who brings the
case and what remedy is sought.
