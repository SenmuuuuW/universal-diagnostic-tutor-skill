# Math Example

## User Question

Why do we flip the inequality sign when multiplying by a negative number?

## Diagnosis

- Subject: Math.
- Topic: Inequalities and order on the number line.
- Prerequisite: Understanding greater than, less than, and multiplication by
  negative numbers.
- Likely gap: The learner may know the rule but not the reason behind it.

## Example Answer Structure

1. Start with a number-line intuition.
2. Show a simple true inequality, such as `2 < 5`.
3. Multiply both sides by `-1` and compare `-2` and `-5`.
4. Explain that multiplication by a negative reverses direction on the number
   line.
5. Give the final rule and one quick practice question.

## Why This Is Diagnosis-First

The answer does not just say "flip the sign." It identifies the missing mental
model: negative multiplication reverses order.
