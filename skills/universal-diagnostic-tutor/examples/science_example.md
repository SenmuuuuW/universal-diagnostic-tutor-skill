# Science Example

## User Question

If Earth is closer to the Sun sometimes, why are seasons not caused by distance?

## Diagnosis

- Subject: Natural science.
- Topic: Astronomy and Earth's seasons.
- Prerequisite: Rotation, orbit, axial tilt, sunlight angle, and hemispheres.
- Likely gap: Confusing orbital distance with solar energy received per area.

## Example Answer Structure

1. State the misconception gently.
2. Explain Earth's axial tilt in plain language.
3. Show how sunlight angle and day length affect heating.
4. Explain why opposite hemispheres have opposite seasons.
5. Mention that Earth is actually near perihelion during Northern Hemisphere
   winter.
6. Give a check question about Australia and the United States.

## Why This Is Diagnosis-First

The answer diagnoses a common misconception before teaching the actual mechanism
behind seasons.
