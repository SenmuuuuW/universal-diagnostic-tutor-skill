# Writing Revision Example

## User Question

Can you make this thesis stronger? "Social media is bad for teenagers."

## Diagnosis

- Subject: Writing and revision.
- Topic: Thesis specificity and arguability.
- Prerequisite: A strong thesis needs a clear claim, scope, reason, and stakes.
- Likely gap: The learner has a broad opinion but not yet a precise argument.

## Teaching Depth Level

Level 3: Standard teacher-style explanation.

## Example Answer

This is a thesis revision task. The issue is not grammar; it is that the claim
is too broad. A stronger thesis should say **which effect**, **on which group**,
and **why it matters**.

Original: "Social media is bad for teenagers."

Stronger version: "Social media can harm teenagers' mental health when platforms
reward constant comparison, because repeated exposure to curated images can
increase anxiety and lower self-esteem."

Why this is stronger:

1. It narrows "bad" to mental health.
2. It explains a cause: constant comparison.
3. It gives a path for evidence: anxiety, self-esteem, and platform design.

If the essay needs a more balanced thesis, try: "Although social media can help
teenagers maintain friendships, its design can harm mental health when it
encourages constant comparison and addictive checking."

Common mistake: Making the thesis sound intense but not specific. Specificity
usually beats volume.

Check yourself: What evidence would you use to prove the revised thesis?

## Why This Is Diagnosis-First

The answer diagnoses the writing problem before rewriting, so the learner sees
how to improve future theses.

## What An Answer-First Assistant Would Miss

It would provide a polished sentence without explaining why the revision is
stronger.
