# Economics And Business Example

## User Question

Why can a company be profitable but still run out of cash?

## Diagnosis

- Subject: Economics and business.
- Topic: Profit vs cash flow.
- Prerequisite: Difference between accounting profit, timing of cash, working
  capital, and expenses already recorded.
- Likely gap: Confusing income statement profit with money currently available.

## Teaching Depth Level

Level 4: Foundation-first full explanation.

## Example Answer

This is a business finance question. The key prerequisite is that **profit and
cash are not the same measurement**.

Profit is usually measured over a period using accounting rules. A company can
record revenue when it earns the sale, even if the customer pays later. Cash is
the money actually available to pay bills today.

Imagine a company sells `$100,000` of products this month and has `$70,000` of
costs, so it shows `$30,000` profit. But if customers will not pay for 60 days,
and payroll is due this week, the company may not have enough cash.

The same thing can happen when inventory, rent deposits, loan payments, or
equipment purchases use cash before profit shows the strain clearly.

Final idea: **Profit tells whether the business model is earning more than it
costs. Cash flow tells whether the business can pay obligations on time.**

Common mistake: Thinking a profitable company is automatically safe. Timing can
break a business even when the income statement looks healthy.

Check yourself: If a store buys inventory in cash today but sells it on credit,
what happens to cash before customers pay?

## Why This Is Diagnosis-First

The answer first diagnoses the measurement confusion, then explains the timing
mechanism.

## What An Answer-First Assistant Would Miss

It might say "because of cash flow" without teaching why cash flow can diverge
from profit.
