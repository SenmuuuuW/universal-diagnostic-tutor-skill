# Humanities Example

## User Question

Was the French Revolution caused by bad kings?

## Diagnosis

- Subject: Humanities and social sciences.
- Topic: Historical causation.
- Prerequisite: Difference between individual causes, structural causes, and
  triggering events.
- Likely gap: Reducing a complex historical event to one personal cause.

## Example Answer Structure

1. Explain that "bad kings" is too simple as a single cause.
2. Separate long-term causes, short-term pressures, and triggers.
3. Discuss social inequality, fiscal crisis, Enlightenment ideas, food prices,
   and political decisions.
4. Explain how leadership mattered within those broader conditions.
5. Give a balanced conclusion.
6. Ask the learner to classify one cause as structural or immediate.

## Why This Is Diagnosis-First

The answer first diagnoses the thinking problem: oversimplified causation. It
then teaches a better historical reasoning framework.
