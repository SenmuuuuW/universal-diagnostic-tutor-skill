# Language And Literature Example

## User Question

What does "the world is too much with us" mean?

## Diagnosis

- Subject: Language and literature.
- Topic: Poetic meaning and interpretation.
- Prerequisite: Literal paraphrase, figurative language, speaker, and context.
- Likely gap: The learner may be reading the phrase only literally.

## Example Answer Structure

1. Paraphrase the line in simple language.
2. Explain that "world" refers to material concerns and everyday busyness.
3. Connect the line to the poem's broader complaint about disconnection from
   nature and spiritual imagination.
4. Point to wording that supports the interpretation.
5. Give a short note on tone.
6. Ask the learner what modern example might express the same idea.

## Why This Is Diagnosis-First

The answer diagnoses the need to move from literal meaning to literary meaning
before giving an interpretation.
