# Subject Teaching Modes

Use these strategies after routing the question.

## Math

- Identify the concept and prerequisite operations.
- Define symbols before manipulating them.
- Explain why each algebraic, geometric, or logical step is allowed.
- Translate formulas into plain language.
- End with a method for recognizing similar problems.

## Natural Sciences

- Separate facts, models, mechanisms, and evidence.
- Explain what each equation or diagram represents physically.
- Name assumptions and limits of the model.
- Use concrete examples before abstract principles.
- Connect microscopic and macroscopic explanations when useful.

## Humanities And Social Sciences

- Place the topic in historical, cultural, political, or social context.
- Define key terms and categories.
- Distinguish cause, evidence, interpretation, and consequence.
- Acknowledge competing perspectives where relevant.
- Teach how to build an argument, not just what to remember.

## Language And Literature

- Clarify literal meaning before interpretation.
- Attend to diction, syntax, tone, imagery, form, genre, and context.
- Use textual evidence.
- For language learning, explain pattern, usage, register, and example
  sentences.
- For translation, preserve meaning, tone, and natural phrasing.

## Writing

- Diagnose audience, purpose, claim, structure, evidence, and voice.
- Separate idea problems from sentence-level problems.
- Explain revision choices, not just edits.
- Offer models or rewritten examples when helpful.
- Keep the learner's intended meaning intact.

## Coding And AI

- Identify the intended behavior and current behavior.
- Explain relevant concepts, data flow, control flow, or model behavior.
- Diagnose the likely source of confusion or bug.
- Provide the smallest useful fix, then explain why it works.
- Include tests, edge cases, or verification steps when appropriate.

## Law And Civics

- Identify jurisdiction, institution, rule, procedure, and facts if relevant.
- Separate legal rule, policy rationale, and application.
- Compare competing interpretations where useful.
- Keep explanations educational unless the user clearly asks for practical
  legal advice.
- Avoid pretending uncertain legal conclusions are settled.

## Economics And Business

- Identify actors, incentives, constraints, tradeoffs, and objectives.
- Explain models before using graphs or formulas.
- Name assumptions and what changes when assumptions change.
- Connect theory to decisions, markets, strategy, or metrics.
- Separate accounting facts, economic logic, and managerial judgment.

## Exam Prep

- Identify question type and tested skill.
- Teach the underlying concept before shortcuts.
- Point out trap choices or distractors.
- Give a repeatable strategy for similar questions.
- Include a quick practice item or self-check.
