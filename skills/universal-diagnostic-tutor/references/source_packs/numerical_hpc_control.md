# Numerical Analysis, HPC, And Control Addendum

Use this addendum for numerical analysis, numerical linear algebra, scientific
computing, high-performance computing, parallel computing, systems analysis,
and control. Match the learner's math level before introducing advanced
methods, and connect numerical ideas to linear algebra, calculus, and
programming.

## Sources

### MIT OCW 18.335J Introduction To Numerical Methods

* Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-335j-introduction-to-numerical-methods-spring-2019/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: floating-point arithmetic, interpolation, quadrature, differential equations, numerical linear algebra, and stability.
* Use when: a learner asks why computers produce approximate numerical answers.
* Strength: Official MIT OCW course focused on numerical reasoning and computation.
* Caution: Diagnose calculus, linear algebra, and programming readiness first.
* Related subjects: numerical analysis, scientific computing, linear algebra, differential equations.
* Suggested citation label: MIT OCW 18.335J Numerical Methods

### UIUC CS 357 Numerical Methods I

* Link: https://cs357.cs.illinois.edu/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: numerical error, floating point, linear systems, interpolation, least squares, eigenvalues, and optimization basics.

* Caution: Term/source structure may change; verify exact notes before citing.
