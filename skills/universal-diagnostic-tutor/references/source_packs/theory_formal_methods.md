# Theory And Formal Methods Addendum

Use this addendum for formal languages, automata, computability, complexity,
logic foundations, and proof-oriented theory questions. Start by diagnosing the
learner's proof maturity before choosing advanced sources.

## Sources

### MIT OCW 6.045J Automata, Computability, And Complexity

* Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-045j-automata-computability-and-complexity-spring-2011/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: finite automata, regular languages, context-free languages, computability, reductions, and complexity classes.
* Use when: a learner asks why automata or computability matter in CS.
* Strength: Official MIT OCW course covering the core theory sequence.
* Caution: Diagnose proof, induction, and set notation gaps before using deeply.
* Related subjects: automata, formal languages, computability, complexity, proofs.
* Suggested citation label: MIT OCW 6.045J Automata And Complexity

### Stanford CS103 Mathematical Foundations Of Computing

* Link: https://web.stanford.edu/class/cs103/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: logic, proofs, sets, functions, induction, automata, regular languages, and computability foundations.
* Use when: a learner needs a beginner-to-intermediate bridge into formal CS.
* Strength: Strong on proof language and conceptual motivation.
* Caution: Course pages can be term-specific; verify exact handouts or lecture pages before citing.
* Related subjects: discrete math, formal languages, logic, computability.
* Suggested citation label: Stanford CS103 Mathematical Foundations

### CMU 15-251 Great Ideas In Theoretical Computer Science

* Link: https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~15251/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: proof ideas, counting, graphs, probability, computability, complexity, and theory intuition.
* Use when: a learner needs broad theory perspective rather than only automata mechanics.
* Strength: Strong conceptual framing of why theory matters.
* Caution: Use specific lecture notes when possible instead of citing only the course homepage.
* Related subjects: theory of computation, discrete structures, probability, complexity.
* Suggested citation label: CMU 15-251 Great Ideas In TCS

### UIUC CS 374 Algorithms And Models Of Computation

* Link: https://courses.grainger.illinois.edu/cs374/sp2024/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: automata, regular languages, context-free languages, computability, reductions, and algorithmic models.
* Use when: a learner needs practice connecting automata theory with algorithmic thinking.
* Strength: Public course site with organized theory and algorithms material.
* Caution: Term-specific page; mark stale if a newer public term is preferred.
* Related subjects: automata, algorithms, complexity, formal languages.
* Suggested citation label: UIUC CS 374

### MIT OCW 6.042J Mathematics For Computer Science

* Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-042j-mathematics-for-computer-science-spring-2015/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: logic, proofs, induction, sets, relations, graphs, counting, and discrete probability.
* Use when: formal language or complexity questions fail because the learner lacks proof prerequisites.
* Strength: Excellent prerequisite repair source for theory-heavy CS.
* Caution: Use before automata/complexity sources when proof fluency is weak.
* Related subjects: discrete math, proofs, algorithms, theory.
* Suggested citation label: MIT OCW 6.042J Mathematics For Computer Science
