# Math Foundations Source Pack

Use these sources as preferred starting points for university-level calculus,
linear algebra, discrete math, probability, statistics, optimization, and
numerical reasoning. Treat them as source candidates, not as an exhaustive list.
Verify links when possible before citing them in a learner-facing answer.

## Sources

### MIT OCW 18.01SC Single Variable Calculus

* Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-01sc-single-variable-calculus-fall-2010/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: limits, derivatives, integrals, series, and calculus problem-solving structure.
* Use when: a learner needs a formal calculus path or wants MIT-style study material.
* Strength: Complete course structure with lectures, examples, and practice resources.
* Caution: Start with intuition before sending a learner into the full course sequence.
* Related subjects: calculus, physics, optimization, machine learning foundations.
* Suggested citation label: MIT OCW 18.01SC Single Variable Calculus

### MIT OCW 18.06 Linear Algebra

* Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-06-linear-algebra-spring-2010/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: matrices, vector spaces, transformations, eigenvalues, orthogonality, and linear systems.
* Use when: explaining how matrices connect to geometry, data, machine learning, graphics, or numerical methods.
* Strength: Clear university course anchor for both conceptual and computational linear algebra.
* Caution: Learners often need a bridge from vectors to matrices before formal vector spaces.
* Related subjects: linear algebra, machine learning, computer graphics, numerical analysis.
* Suggested citation label: MIT OCW 18.06 Linear Algebra

### MIT OCW 18.05 Introduction To Probability And Statistics

* Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-05-introduction-to-probability-and-statistics-spring-2022/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: probability models, random variables, distributions, estimation, and statistical inference.
* Use when: a learner asks about uncertainty, data, ML evaluation, hypothesis testing, or probabilistic reasoning.
* Strength: Strong bridge from probability basics to statistics and data science.
* Caution: Check whether the learner needs counting and algebra refreshers first.
* Related subjects: probability, statistics, machine learning, data mining, experiments.
* Suggested citation label: MIT OCW 18.05 Probability And Statistics

### MIT OCW 6.042J Mathematics For Computer Science

* Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-042j-mathematics-for-computer-science-spring-2015/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: logic, proofs, induction, sets, relations, graphs, counting, and discrete probability.
* Use when: a CS learner is stuck on proof style, recurrence reasoning, graph ideas, or discrete structures.
* Strength: Directly connects mathematical foundations to computer science thinking.
* Caution: Proof-heavy material may require slower diagnosis of notation and logic gaps.
* Related subjects: discrete math, algorithms, theory, data structures, probability.
* Suggested citation label: MIT OCW 6.042J Mathematics For Computer Science

### OpenStax Calculus Volume 1

* Link: https://openstax.org/details/books/calculus-volume-1
* Type: open textbook
* Best for: accessible calculus explanations, examples, and practice problems.
* Use when: a learner needs textbook-style support for single-variable calculus.
* Strength: Open textbook with clear sections that are easy to assign for follow-up reading.
* Caution: Use only short references and summaries; do not copy textbook content.
* Related subjects: calculus, physics, engineering mathematics, optimization.
* Suggested citation label: OpenStax Calculus Volume 1

### OpenStax Introductory Statistics 2e

* Link: https://openstax.org/details/books/introductory-statistics-2e
* Type: open textbook
* Best for: descriptive statistics, probability distributions, confidence intervals, and hypothesis tests.
* Use when: a learner needs a structured statistics reference with practice.
* Strength: Good for fundamentals before higher-level data science or ML evaluation.
* Caution: It is introductory; use university notes for more advanced probability or mathematical statistics.
* Related subjects: statistics, data analysis, experiments, machine learning evaluation.
* Suggested citation label: OpenStax Introductory Statistics 2e

### Stanford EE364a Convex Optimization

* Link: https://web.stanford.edu/class/ee364a/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: convex sets, convex functions, optimization problems, duality, and applications.
* Use when: a learner asks why optimization matters in ML, control, signals, or engineering design.
* Strength: Authoritative advanced source for optimization foundations.
* Caution: Too advanced for beginners; diagnose linear algebra and calculus readiness first.
* Related subjects: optimization, machine learning, control, signal processing, numerical analysis.
* Suggested citation label: Stanford EE364a Convex Optimization

### 3Blue1Brown Essence Of Linear Algebra

* Link: https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDMsr9K-rj53DwVRMYO3t5Yr
* Type: tutorial
* Best for: visual intuition about vectors, matrices, transformations, determinants, and eigenvectors.
* Use when: the learner understands formulas but cannot picture what they mean.
* Strength: Excellent intuition-builder before or alongside formal sources.
* Caution: Treat as visual intuition, not a replacement for a formal course or textbook.
* Related subjects: linear algebra, machine learning, graphics, transformations.
* Suggested citation label: 3Blue1Brown Essence Of Linear Algebra
