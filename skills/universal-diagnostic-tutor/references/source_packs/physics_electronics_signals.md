# Physics, Electronics, And Signals Source Pack

Use these sources for mechanics, electromagnetism, circuits, digital logic,
signals, systems, signal processing, controls, and the physical layer beneath
computing. Diagnose whether the learner needs physical intuition, mathematical
modeling, or engineering implementation first.

## Sources

### MIT OCW 8.01SC Classical Mechanics

* Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/8-01sc-classical-mechanics-fall-2016/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: kinematics, forces, energy, momentum, rotation, and mechanics problem solving.
* Use when: a learner needs a rigorous physics foundation for engineering or signals later.
* Strength: Complete mechanics course with structured learning materials.
* Caution: Separate conceptual physics gaps from calculus or vector gaps.
* Related subjects: physics, engineering, mechanics, calculus applications.
* Suggested citation label: MIT OCW 8.01SC Classical Mechanics

### OpenStax University Physics Volume 1

* Link: https://openstax.org/details/books/university-physics-volume-1
* Type: open textbook
* Best for: mechanics, waves, and introductory university physics explanations.
* Use when: a learner needs readable textbook support and practice problems.
* Strength: Open and structured for broad university physics foundations.
* Caution: Cite sections rather than copying textbook content.
* Related subjects: physics, mechanics, waves, engineering foundations.
* Suggested citation label: OpenStax University Physics Volume 1

### OpenStax University Physics Volume 2

* Link: https://openstax.org/details/books/university-physics-volume-2
* Type: open textbook
* Best for: thermodynamics, electricity and magnetism, circuits, and optics.
* Use when: a learner asks how physical signals, circuits, fields, or waves work.
* Strength: Accessible open textbook for E&M and circuit-related foundations.
* Caution: Pair with circuit or signals sources for engineering design questions.
* Related subjects: electromagnetism, electronics, circuits, optics, physics.
* Suggested citation label: OpenStax University Physics Volume 2

### MIT OCW 6.002 Circuits And Electronics

* Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-002-circuits-and-electronics-spring-2007/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: circuit laws, transistors, small-signal models, amplifiers, digital abstraction, and electronics foundations.
* Use when: a learner asks how signals become measurable or computable in circuits.
* Strength: University-level bridge from physics to electronics and computation.
* Caution: Diagnose algebra, differential equations, and circuit notation readiness.
* Related subjects: electronics, circuits, digital logic, signals, computer engineering.
* Suggested citation label: MIT OCW 6.002 Circuits And Electronics

### MIT OCW 6.004 Computation Structures

* Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-004-computation-structures-spring-2017/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: digital logic, gates, finite-state machines, processors, assembly, and computer organization.
* Use when: a learner asks how electronics become logic and how logic becomes computation.
* Strength: Strong connection from digital circuits to computer architecture.
* Caution: Keep analog electronics separate from digital abstraction unless both are needed.
* Related subjects: digital logic, computer organization, architecture, electronics.
* Suggested citation label: MIT OCW 6.004 Computation Structures

### MIT OCW 6.003 Signals And Systems

* Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-003-signals-and-systems-fall-2011/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: signals, systems, convolution, frequency response, Fourier ideas, and system behavior.
* Use when: a learner asks why time-domain and frequency-domain views both matter.
* Strength: Core engineering source for signal and system intuition.
* Caution: Learners may need calculus, complex numbers, and linear systems reviewed first.
* Related subjects: signal processing, control, electronics, communications, physics.
* Suggested citation label: MIT OCW 6.003 Signals And Systems

### MIT OCW 6.341 Discrete-Time Signal Processing

* Link: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-341-discrete-time-signal-processing-fall-2005/
* Type: university open course
* Best for: sampling, discrete-time signals, transforms, filtering, spectra, and DSP algorithms.
* Use when: a learner asks how continuous signals become digital data or how filters work in computation.
* Strength: Authoritative DSP course material for advanced signal questions.
* Caution: Advanced; diagnose 6.003-style signals and complex number readiness first.
* Related subjects: digital signal processing, image processing, audio, communications.
* Suggested citation label: MIT OCW 6.341 Discrete-Time Signal Processing
